What is Sago

Sago is a language built from the ground up — not from any natural language, but from the 65 semantic primitives found in every human language ever studied.

🧠

Meaning-first

Every word is built from concepts that are universal — present in every language on earth. No cultural cargo, no borrowed vocabulary.

🎵

90 phonics

90 semantic units, each a two-letter syllable. They combine into flowing words: na+fu → nafu (cold), fu+mo → fumo (fire).

🌍

Truly universal

Based on 50 years of cross-linguistic research by Wierzbicka and Goddard across 90+ languages. Not Eurocentric — not any-centric.

📖

Open forever

The language spec is CC BY. The dictionary is CC0 — public domain. The code is MIT. Use it for anything.

The 90 Phonics PHONICS.md

Every phonic is a CV syllable — one consonant, one vowel. 18 consonants × 5 vowels = exactly 90. The phonological and semantic spaces are in perfect bijection. Click any phonic to learn more.

Grammar

Cognitive order = phonic order. Phonic sequences follow how attention moves: when → who → what → to what → how.

Sentence structure

[TIME] TOPIC PREDICATE [PATIENT]
sa wu duI want water — SELF WANT WATER

No copula

Topic + state = "is"
sa goI am well — SELF GOOD

Tense

pa = past · ya = future · (none) = now
pa sa mo laI went away — BEFORE SELF MOVE FAR

Negation

na directly before what it negates
sa na wu duI don't want water — SELF NOT-WANT WATER

Questions

mi at end (yes/no) · WH-phonic first
to wu du miDo you want water? wa to mo miWhere are you going?

Phonics flow

CV + CV → no clusters, ever
na·fu → nafucold — NOT HEAT fu·mo → fumofire — HEAT MOVE

What the phonics reveal

These compositions were not designed. They emerged when the primitive system was applied to familiar concepts.

nadena
na · de · na — NOT · DO · NOT
Freedom: the absence of the prevention of action. Double negation = liberation.
Berlin's negative liberty in 3 phonics
tesunavi
te · su · na · vi — THINK · TRUE · NOT · SEE
Faith: believing truly in the absence of seeing.
Hebrews 11:1 — "evidence of things not seen"
tere
te · re — THINK · AIR
Spirit: the breath-mind. The connection encoded in spiritus, ruach, pneuma, qi, prana.
Rediscovered across all cultures
dejeba
de · je · ba · bi — DO · TOGETHER · FEEL · BAD
Courage: acting while afraid. Not the absence of fear — presence of action despite it.
Aristotle's definition of courage
wepaka
we · pa · ka — SAY · BEFORE · MANY
History: the many sayings of the past. Not what happened — what was told.
Hayden White's narrativism, 3 phonics
wuyago
wu · ya · go — WANT · AFTER · GOOD
Hope: wanting future good.
Aristotle's definition of hope

Contribute

Sago needs a speaker community to stabilize its conventions. Every language lives through use.

1

Propose a word

Open a pull request adding an entry to language/concepts.json. Include the Sago word, its phonic sequence, and a language-neutral concept description.

2

Build or validate a word

Use the word builder: python3 tools/word_builder.py "your concept" or --validate "nafu" to check any composition.

3

Report a collision

If two concepts share the same Sago word, or the same concept gets two words, open an issue. Disambiguation is the community's job.

4

Translate something

Try the bidirectional translator: python3 tools/translate.py. Share your results — back-translation tests help stabilize conventions.
