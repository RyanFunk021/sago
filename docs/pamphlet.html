A language built entirely from the atoms of human meaning

Every language hides its meaning inside words that evolved by accident. Sago starts from meaning itself — 90 universal semantic units, each a single spoken syllable, composing everything a person needs to say.

Why Sago

Language shouldn't require a shared accident of history

Wierzbicka's Natural Semantic Metalanguage research identified ~65 semantic primes — concepts like SELF, WANT, GOOD, THINK — that appear in every human language with no simpler equivalent. They are the irreducible atoms of meaning. Sago promotes them from a research tool to a spoken language.

Because every phonic maps to a universal concept, Sago is learnable by speakers of any language without privileging the structure of English, Mandarin, or Arabic. The meaning comes first; the words follow.

Key Properties

Four design constraints

1
Phonological completeness 18 consonants × 5 vowels = exactly 90 CV syllables = exactly 90 semantic units. No syllable is wasted or ambiguous.
2
Natural flow Every phonic ends in a vowel, every phonic starts with a consonant — so every boundary is a smooth V+C junction. No consonant clusters, ever.
3
Tiered acquisition 8 phonics cover basic survival. 16 cover daily life. 32 cover nuanced grammar. You can begin speaking on day one.
4
Cross-linguistic validity Every phonic corresponds to a concept verified across 90+ languages. Nothing is borrowed from English etymology or European grammar.

Grammar in Brief

Five rules cover most sentences

1 Word order: [TIME] TOPIC VERB [OBJECT] — no exceptions, no agreement.
2 No copula: sa go = "I am good." Topic + state = "is."
3 Tense by prefix: pa = past, ya = future. No tense marker = present.
4 Negation: na immediately before what it negates. sa na wu = "I don't want."
5 Questions: mi at end for yes/no. WH-phonic first for content. ze to wu mi? = "What do you want?"
6 Causation: da = because/so. sa mo da sa wu du = "I move because I want water."
7 Flow: Phonics concatenate directly — na + fu → nafu (cold), fu + mo → fumo (fire). No clusters.
How to Read Sago

Each syllable is a concept

Read Sago left to right, one CV syllable at a time. Each syllable is one atom of meaning.

sa   ba   go   ma   to
SELF · FEEL · GOOD · MORE · OTHER
"I love you"
ka   pu   ba   go   je
MANY · PEOPLE · FEEL · GOOD · TOGETHER
"Peace"

Phonics can fuse into flowing words: nafu (cold), fumo (fire), bago (joy). Because every phonic ends in a vowel, adjacent phonics always connect smoothly — Sago flows like water.
